# 7-Day Road Trip: Yellowstone & Grand Teton National Parks

**Route:** Cupertino, CA → Yellowstone National Park → Grand Teton National Park → Cupertino, CA  
**Travelers:** 2 Adults, 2 Kids  
**Total Drive Distance:** ~2,000 miles (round trip)  
**Best Time to Visit:** Late June – Early September (roads fully open, weather family-friendly)

---

## Trip Overview

| Day | Route | Highlights |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Cupertino → Twin Falls, ID | Shoshone Falls, Nevada desert scenery |
| 2 | Twin Falls → Grand Teton NP | Scenic Idaho drive, Jenny Lake arrival |
| 3 | Grand Teton NP (Full Day) | Jenny Lake, Mormon Row, wildlife watching |
| 4 | Grand Teton → Yellowstone NP | Colter Bay, West Thumb Geyser Basin, Old Faithful |
| 5 | Yellowstone (Full Day) | Grand Prismatic Spring, Norris Geyser Basin, Grand Canyon of Yellowstone |
| 6 | Yellowstone (Full Day) | Hayden Valley, Lamar Valley, Yellowstone Lake, Mud Volcano |
| 7 | Yellowstone → Cupertino | Scenic drive back via Salt Lake City |

---

## Packing Essentials for Families

- Layered clothing (temperatures can swing 40°F between morning and afternoon)
- Sturdy walking shoes or hiking boots for all family members
- Sunscreen, insect repellent, and bear spray (available for purchase/rental in park)
- Binoculars for wildlife watching
- National Parks Annual Pass ("America the Beautiful" – covers entry fees for all)
- Snacks, reusable water bottles, and a cooler
- Camera / extra memory cards
- First-aid kit
- Kids' activity journals / Junior Ranger booklets (free at visitor centers)

## Day 1 — Cupertino to Twin Falls, Idaho

**Drive:** ~620 miles | ~9 hours (with stops)  
**Overnight:** Twin Falls, ID

### Morning – Hit the Road Early (6:00 AM)
- Depart Cupertino via **I-580 E → I-80 E**
- Fuel up and grab coffee/breakfast to go
- Drive through Sacramento and the Sierra Nevada foothills

### Afternoon – Nevada Desert Drive
- Pass through **Reno, NV** – great rest stop with restaurants (~3 hrs from Cupertino)
- Continue east on **I-80** through the Nevada high desert
- Stop in **Elko, NV** for lunch (~5.5 hrs from Cupertino)
  - Kid-friendly option: McDonald's, Pizza Barn, or The Star Hotel

### Late Afternoon – Twin Falls, Idaho
- Exit I-80 at Wells and head north on US-93 to Twin Falls (~1.5 hrs from Elko)
- Visit **Shoshone Falls** 🌊 – called the "Niagara of the West" (taller than Niagara!)
  - Free to visit; spectacular viewpoints
  - Kids love the mist and power of the falls
- Walk the **Dierkes Lake Park** area next to the falls (easy, flat paths)
- Explore the **Perrine Bridge** over the Snake River gorge

### Evening
- Check into hotel in Twin Falls
- Dinner at **Rock Creek Restaurant** or **The Anchor Bistro**

> 💡 **Tip:** Download offline Google Maps for Idaho and Wyoming before departure. Cell service can be spotty near the parks.

## Day 2 — Twin Falls to Grand Teton National Park

**Drive:** ~230 miles | ~3.5 hours  
**Overnight:** Jackson, WY or Moose, WY area

### Morning – Idaho Falls & Snake River Plain
- Depart Twin Falls via **US-93 N → I-15 N**
- Drive through the scenic Snake River Plain
- Stop in **Idaho Falls, ID** for breakfast (~1.5 hrs from Twin Falls)
  - Try **Smoky Mountain Pizza** or grab groceries for park picnics
- Cross into Wyoming – the Teton Range begins to appear on the horizon 🏔️

### Afternoon – Entering Grand Teton National Park
- Arrive via the **Moose entrance** (south entrance off Hwy 26/89/191)
- Stop at **Craig Thomas Discovery & Visitor Center** (Moose)
  - Kids pick up their **Junior Ranger booklets** (free!)
  - Excellent interactive exhibits on geology, wildlife, and Native history
- Drive the **Teton Park Road** – jaw-dropping views of the Cathedral Group peaks
- Stop at **Oxbow Bend** – one of the most photographed spots in Wyoming
  - Reflections of Mt. Moran in the Snake River
  - Watch for moose, bald eagles, and great blue herons
- **Jackson Lake Dam & Overlook** – panoramic views of Jackson Lake and the Tetons

### Evening
- Check into lodging near Jackson or inside the park
  - **Kid-friendly options:** Signal Mountain Lodge, Colter Bay Cabins, or Jackson hotels
- Dinner at the lodge or drive to **Jackson Town Square**
  - Try **Bubba's Bar-B-Que** or **Pinky G's Pizzeria** (both family favorites)
  - Walk the Town Square with the famous elk antler arches

> 💡 **Tip:** Book lodging inside or near Grand Teton months in advance. The area fills up quickly in summer.

## Day 3 — Grand Teton National Park (Full Day)

**Overnight:** Jackson / Grand Teton area  
**Total Miles in Park:** ~40 miles

### Morning – Jenny Lake Area ⭐ (Must-See)
- **Jenny Lake Visitor Center** – geology and climbing exhibits
- **Jenny Lake Shuttle Boat** 🚤 – take the boat across Jenny Lake ($)
  - Saves 2 miles of walking each way; kids love it!
  - Hike to **Hidden Falls** (0.5 miles from boat dock) – a stunning 200-ft waterfall
  - Continue to **Inspiration Point** (another 0.5 miles up) – panoramic views of the lake and valley
  - Trail is well-marked and doable for kids 6+ with some encouragement
- **String Lake** – easy, flat loop trail; great for younger kids
  - Swimming allowed in summer (cold but refreshing!)
  - Excellent views of the Tetons directly above

### Midday – Picnic Lunch
- Pack a picnic to eat at **String Lake picnic area** or **Jenny Lake Overlook**
- Keep food in bear-proof containers (required in the park)

### Afternoon – Valley Floor & Historic Sites
- **Snake River Overlook** 📷 – iconic viewpoint immortalized by Ansel Adams
  - The full Teton Range reflected in the Snake River on calm mornings
- **Mormon Row Historic District** 🏚️ – family favorite!
  - Historic homestead barns framed by the Teton peaks
  - Bison often graze in the fields – stay 25 yards away
  - Great photo opportunity
- **Schwabacher Landing** – peaceful riverside meadow
  - Look for moose feeding in the beaver ponds at dawn/dusk
  - Easy, flat walk on a dirt road

### Evening – Wildlife Watching (Prime Time)
- Drive **Antelope Flats Road** at dusk for pronghorn and bison sightings
- Return to **Oxbow Bend** – moose are most active at dawn and dusk
- Wildlife commonly spotted in Grand Teton:
  - 🦌 Moose, elk, pronghorn antelope
  - 🐻 Black bears and grizzly bears (keep distance!)
  - 🦅 Bald eagles, ospreys, trumpeter swans
  - 🦬 American bison
- Dinner at **Signal Mountain Lodge** restaurant (lake views) or back in Jackson

> 💡 **Junior Ranger Program:** Encourage kids to complete their Grand Teton Junior Ranger booklets today. They earn an official badge at the visitor center!

## Day 4 — Grand Teton to Yellowstone National Park

**Drive (within parks):** ~60 miles | ~1.5–2 hours  
**Overnight:** Old Faithful area or West Yellowstone, MT

### Morning – Final Grand Teton Highlights
- **Colter Bay Visitor Center & Indian Arts Museum** (north end of Grand Teton)
  - Excellent Native American artifact collection; great for older kids
  - Short lakeside walk to Colter Bay marina
- **Signal Mountain Summit Road** 🚗 – 5-mile drive to the summit
  - 360° panoramic views of Jackson Hole valley and all four mountain ranges
  - No RVs allowed; perfect for family cars

### Midday – Drive North into Yellowstone
- Travel north through the **John D. Rockefeller Jr. Memorial Parkway**
- Enter Yellowstone via the **South Entrance**
- Stop at the **South Entrance Ranger Station** – pick up Yellowstone Junior Ranger booklets!
- **Lewis Lake** – scenic lake stop; great for a quick picnic

### Afternoon – West Thumb & Old Faithful
- **West Thumb Geyser Basin** ♨️
  - Geothermal features right on the shore of Yellowstone Lake
  - Includes the famous "Fishing Cone" (historically used to cook fish!)
  - Easy boardwalk loop (~0.5 miles); safe and fascinating for kids
- Drive the **Grand Loop Road** west toward Old Faithful
- **Kepler Cascades** – short roadside waterfall stop

### Evening – Old Faithful ⭐ (Must-See)
- **Old Faithful Geyser** 🌋 – Yellowstone's iconic geyser
  - Erupts approximately every 60–110 minutes, ~1.5–2 minutes per eruption
  - Check predicted eruption times at the visitor center
  - Boardwalk area safely accommodates the whole family
- **Old Faithful Visitor Education Center** – interactive exhibits on geothermal activity
  - Kids can watch a film on geysers (fun and educational)
- Walk the **Upper Geyser Basin** boardwalk (~1.5 miles round trip)
  - Includes Castle Geyser, Beehive Geyser, Grand Geyser, and Morning Glory Pool
- **Old Faithful Inn** – even if not staying, walk through this historic 1904 log hotel
  - The "Old House" lobby with its 76-foot volcanic rock fireplace is spectacular
- Dinner at **Old Faithful Inn Dining Room** or **Old Faithful Lodge** cafeteria

> 💡 **Tip:** Arrive at Old Faithful 30 minutes before the predicted eruption to get a good boardwalk seat.

## Day 5 — Yellowstone: Geysers, Hot Springs & the Grand Canyon

**Overnight:** Canyon Village or Mammoth area  
**Total Miles in Park:** ~60 miles

### Morning – Midway Geyser Basin ⭐ (Must-See)
- **Grand Prismatic Spring** 🌈 – the largest hot spring in the USA (and 3rd largest in the world)
  - Vivid rainbow colors from heat-loving bacteria (blue center, then yellow, orange, red edges)
  - Boardwalk loops around the spring
- **Excelsior Geyser Crater** – massive 200×300 ft boiling crater draining into Firehole River
- **Fairy Falls Trail** (2.4 miles round trip from trailhead)
  - Hike through lodgepole pine forest to a 197-ft waterfall
  - **Grand Prismatic Overlook** on this trail – the best aerial view of the spring's colors!
  - Doable for kids 7+ at a relaxed pace

### Midday – Fountain Paint Pot Area
- **Fountain Paint Pot** ♨️ – one of Yellowstone's most varied thermal areas
  - All four types of thermal features in one loop: geysers, hot springs, fumaroles (steam vents), and mud pots
  - The bubbling, gurgling mud pots absolutely delight kids
- **Firehole Lake Drive** (scenic 3-mile one-way loop)
  - Great Fountain Geyser (can erupt 100–220 ft high; check schedule)
  - White Dome Geyser, Pink Cone Geyser
- **Firehole River Swimming Area** 🏊 – one of the few places in Yellowstone where swimming is allowed
  - Warm (not hot) water heated by thermal runoff
  - Kids love this – bring swimsuits!

### Afternoon – Norris Geyser Basin
- **Norris Geyser Basin** ♨️ – the hottest, most acidic, and most dynamic geyser basin in the park
  - **Steamboat Geyser** – the tallest active geyser in the world (eruptions up to 300+ ft)
  - **Echinus Geyser** – one of the largest acid-water geysers
  - **Porcelain Basin** – otherworldly landscape of white silica, colored pools, and steam
  - Two boardwalk loops: Porcelain Basin (0.5 miles) and Back Basin (1.5 miles)

### Evening – Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone ⭐ (Must-See)
- **Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone** 🏞️ – 20 miles long, up to 1,200 ft deep, vivid yellow walls
- **Upper Falls Viewpoint** – see the 109-ft Upper Falls
- **Lower Falls Viewpoint** – the 308-ft Lower Falls (almost twice Niagara's height!)
- **Artist Point** 📷 – THE iconic Yellowstone view; the massive Lower Falls framed by the yellow canyon walls
- **Lookout Point & Red Rock Point** – different angles of the Lower Falls (some stairs)
- Check in at **Canyon Village** for the night
- Dinner at Canyon Village Lodge or cafeteria

> 💡 **Junior Ranger:** Yellowstone's Junior Ranger program has multiple age levels. Be sure the kids are working through their activity books each evening!

## Day 6 — Yellowstone: Wildlife, Lakes & Northern Wonders

**Overnight:** Canyon Village or Mammoth Hot Springs  
**Total Miles in Park:** ~70 miles

### Morning – Hayden Valley (Best Wildlife Viewing) ⭐
- **Hayden Valley** 🦬 – the premier wildlife-watching area in Yellowstone
  - Large bison herds frequently block the road (be patient – it's worth it!)
  - Grizzly bears, wolves, coyotes, ravens, and trumpeter swans regularly seen
  - Early morning (7–9 AM) is peak wildlife activity time
  - Pullouts all along the valley for safe viewing; bring binoculars
- **Mud Volcano Area** ♨️
  - **Dragon's Mouth Spring** – a cave with churning, roaring hot water (kids love the drama!)
  - **Mud Volcano** itself – turbulent, boiling mud
  - **Black Dragon's Cauldron** – massive, noisy mud pool
  - Short boardwalk loop (~0.7 miles)

### Midday – Yellowstone Lake
- **Fishing Bridge** – historic bridge where anglers once fished (now protected for cutthroat trout)
  - **Fishing Bridge Visitor Center** – excellent wildlife exhibits; taxidermied animals
- **Yellowstone Lake** 🏔️ – the largest high-elevation lake in North America
  - Elevation 7,733 ft; stunning views of the Absaroka Mountains
  - **Lake Village** – explore the historic **Lake Yellowstone Hotel** (1891), the oldest standing building in the park
- Picnic lunch at a lake-side area

### Afternoon – Lamar Valley (Wolf & Bear Country) ⭐
- Drive north toward **Tower-Roosevelt** area
- **Tower Fall** – 132-ft waterfall with distinctive volcanic "towers" above it
  - Short 0.4-mile trail to the base (steep but worth it)
- **Lamar Valley** 🐺 – called the "Serengeti of North America"
  - THE best place in the world to see wolves in the wild
  - Large bison herds, grizzly bears, pronghorn, coyotes
  - Wolf-watchers gather at dawn and dusk with spotting scopes
- **Petrified Tree** – a 50-million-year-old petrified redwood stump preserved in the volcanic rock

### Evening – Mammoth Hot Springs ⭐
- **Mammoth Hot Springs Terraces** ♨️ – unique travertine terrace formations
  - Unlike silica-based geysers, these are limestone terraces colored by thermophile bacteria
  - **Minerva Terrace**, **Palette Spring**, **Liberty Cap** (a 37-ft dormant hot spring cone)
  - Upper and Lower Terrace boardwalk loops
  - Constantly changing – these terraces look different year to year
- **Mammoth Village** – watch for elk grazing on the hotel lawns (they're remarkably tolerant of people)
- Dinner at **Mammoth Hot Springs Hotel Dining Room** or Terrace Grill

> 💡 **Tip:** The Lamar Valley wolves are most reliably seen at dawn and dusk. Join wolf-watchers with spotting scopes for the best experience – they're incredibly generous about sharing views!

## Day 7 — Yellowstone to Cupertino

**Drive:** ~970 miles | ~14–15 hours (split into two legs with a substantial midday break)  
**Overnight:** Home in Cupertino!

> ⚠️ **Note:** This is a long drive day. Depart by 5:00–6:00 AM to arrive home at a reasonable hour. Consider stopping for a proper sit-down meal break to rest.

### Early Morning – Depart Yellowstone
- Exit Yellowstone via the **West Entrance** (West Yellowstone, MT) for the fastest return route
- OR exit via **South Entrance** back through Grand Teton for a slightly scenic route

### Morning – Drive South Through Idaho
- Head south on **US-20** through Idaho Falls to **I-15 S**
- OR take **US-89 S** through Logan Canyon, Utah (scenic, adds ~30 min)
- **Pocatello → Idaho Falls → I-15 South** toward Utah

### Midday – Salt Lake City, Utah
- Arrive in **Salt Lake City** (~4 hours from Yellowstone)
- Lunch break – plenty of family restaurant options
  - Try **Crown Burger** (local Utah favorite) or **Red Robin**
- Quick stop opportunity: **Utah State Capitol Building** grounds (kids enjoy walking around)
- Continue south on **I-15** then west on **I-80 W** into Nevada

### Afternoon – Nevada Highway
- Cross into Nevada on **I-80 W**
- **Elko, NV** – rest stop (~3 hours from Salt Lake City)
- Continue west through **Battle Mountain**, **Winnemucca**, **Lovelock**

### Evening – Home Stretch to Cupertino
- **Reno, NV** (~2.5 hours from Elko) – last major fuel/dinner stop
  - Dinner at **Squeeze In** or any restaurant along the strip
- Cross the Sierra Nevada via **I-80 W** (Donner Pass/Truckee)
  - Scenic mountain pass – beautiful even at night
- Drop into the Bay Area via Sacramento → I-680 or I-880 to Cupertino
- **Arrive home in Cupertino** 🏠

## Wildlife Viewing Tips for Families

- **Best times:** Dawn (6–9 AM) and dusk (6–9 PM) are peak wildlife activity times
- **Keep distance:** 25 yards from bison/elk; 100 yards from bears and wolves (use binoculars!)
- **Bear safety:** Carry bear spray and know how to use it; make noise on trails; never run
- **Common sightings:** Bison are everywhere; moose are most common near willows/water
- **Wolves:** Best odds in Lamar Valley early morning; ask rangers for recent sightings
- **Bears:** Hayden Valley and Lamar Valley are prime grizzly country

## Important Park Information

- **Yellowstone National Park Entrance Fee:** $35/vehicle (7 days) OR use America the Beautiful Pass ($80/year)
- **Grand Teton National Park Entrance Fee:** $35/vehicle (7 days) OR same annual pass
- **Junior Ranger Program:** Free at all visitor centers; kids earn badges for completing activity books
- **Cell Service:** Very limited inside both parks – download offline maps and save key phone numbers before entry
- **Gas:** Fill up in Jackson, WY or West Yellowstone, MT before entering the parks – limited gas stations inside
- **Food:** Pack snacks and picnic supplies; in-park dining is limited and expensive
- **Altitude:** Both parks are above 6,000 ft elevation; stay hydrated and watch for altitude effects in kids
- **Weather:** Summer afternoons bring frequent afternoon thunderstorms; start hikes early and carry rain gear
- **Wildlife Jams:** Budget extra time – bison traffic jams are common and can add 30–60 minutes
